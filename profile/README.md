## 💡 Overview

<a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">Celemony Melodyne Studio</a> is the most capable version of Melodyne — the professional pitch and timing editing tool that has become essential in music production for vocal correction, creative pitch manipulation, and the remarkable DNA Direct Note Access technology that enables editing individual notes within polyphonic audio recordings.

The <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">DNA Direct Note Access</a> is Melodyne's most significant technical innovation — the ability to identify and edit individual notes within a polyphonic audio recording such as a piano chord, guitar strumming, or any audio containing multiple simultaneous pitches. Where standard pitch editing tools see a mono pitch at each moment, Melodyne's DNA algorithm analyzes the audio and separates the overlapping frequency content into identifiable individual notes, each of which manipulates independently. Adjusting the pitch of one note within a chord, changing the timing of a specific guitar string's attack, or restructuring the harmony of a recorded chord progression — these operations were technically impossible in audio editing before Melodyne's DNA technology. The <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">note-level blob editing</a> displays audio as discrete pitch objects — "blobs" — each representing a note event in the recording. Pitch corrects by dragging blobs to the target pitch, timing adjusts by repositioning blobs along the timeline, note duration extends or shortens by stretching blob edges, and each note's characteristics (vibrato, pitch drift, formant) adjust through dedicated editing handles on each blob.

The <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">formant control</a> shifts the formant frequencies of audio independently from the pitch — raising or lowering the perceived vocal or instrument character without changing the musical pitch, or maintaining natural formant position when pitch-shifting beyond what sounds natural without formant adjustment. The <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">rhythmic quantization</a> aligns note timing to a grid while preserving natural feel — the intelligent algorithm maintains groove and feel characteristics while correcting timing errors that fall outside acceptable range.

<a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">Tempo and time stretching</a> adjusts audio timing across the complete range from subtle groove correction to extreme time-based creative effects — stretching and compressing audio events with Melodyne's high-quality time stretching algorithm that maintains audio quality across significant tempo changes. <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">Vibrato editing controls</a> shape vibrato characteristics on notes — adjusting the rate, depth, and timing of natural vibrato, reducing excessive vibrato, or adding vibrato to straight notes through Melodyne's modulation tools.

<a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">ARA plugin integration</a> connects Melodyne Studio directly into compatible DAWs — Logic Pro, Ableton Live, Pro Tools, and others — through the Audio Random Access protocol that allows the DAW to pass audio to Melodyne for direct editing within the DAW session without audio export and import. <a href="#celemonyMelodyneStudio">Standalone application mode</a> operates Melodyne without a DAW for focused pitch editing sessions that don't require the complete production environment.

## 📌 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What is DNA Direct Note Access?</summary>

DNA is Melodyne's technology for identifying and editing individual notes within polyphonic audio — adjusting one note in a chord or one instrument within a recording containing multiple simultaneous pitches.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the difference between Melodyne editions?</summary>

Melodyne comes in Essential, Assistant, Editor, and Studio editions. Studio is the most capable, with DNA Direct Note Access, multi-track editing, and the Sound Editor. Check the Celemony website for the current edition comparison.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Melodyne work as a plugin in Logic Pro?</summary>

Yes. Melodyne integrates into Logic Pro and other compatible DAWs through the ARA protocol for direct in-session audio editing without file export.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Melodyne correct pitch in real time?</summary>

Melodyne is primarily an offline editing tool — audio loads for analysis and editing before rendering. For real-time pitch correction, tools like Celemony's Tune are more appropriate.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Melodyne edit timing as well as pitch?</summary>

Yes. Note positions adjust for timing correction and groove manipulation, with rhythmic quantization available for aligning timing to a grid.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Melodyne control vibrato?</summary>

Yes. Vibrato editing controls the rate, depth, and timing of vibrato on individual notes.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Melodyne generate harmonies?</summary>

Yes. Pitch-shifting copies of a performance to harmony pitches with formant adjustment for natural-sounding harmony generation from a single recorded performance.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Melodyne Studio support multi-track editing?</summary>

Yes. Studio edition edits multiple tracks simultaneously in a single Melodyne instance — vocal stacks, instrumental parts, and complete song sections together.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Melodyne run on Apple Silicon?</summary>

Yes. Current versions support Apple Silicon Macs natively.

</details>
